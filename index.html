About Me

I'm an undergraduate student pursuing honors in biology at NYU, currently working in the Gresham Lab. The lab uses budding yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae) as a model organism to study how evolutionary adaptations arise in response to changes in the environment.

This summer I completed a research-intensive bootcamp that gave me hands-on training in core wet-lab techniques: gel electrophoresis, PCR, yeast transformations, and flow cytometry. These skills have given me the foundation to begin an independent research project in the lab.

Alongside the bench work, I've been learning R for data analysis and visualization — applying statistical concepts like simulation and the Central Limit Theorem to real scientific questions.
